Securitisation Sector Dashboard EU/UK Market

Interactive visualisation of ~50 roles across the European securitisation ecosystem, representing an estimated — professionals. Each rectangle's area is proportional to estimated headcount. Colour shows the selected metric — toggle between AI Exposure, Salary, Automation Risk, and Regulatory Complexity.

The securitisation value chain spans 10 sectors: from loan origination and structuring through legal, credit rating, compliance, servicing, trading, technology, due diligence, and operations. Roles are scored on three dimensions that capture how AI and regulation will reshape the industry.

Inspiration & methodology comparison with karpathy.ai/jobs
This dashboard is inspired by Andrej Karpathy's US Job Market Visualizer (github.com/karpathy/jobs), which visualises 342 BLS occupations covering 143M US jobs with AI exposure scores. We adapted the concept, treemap visualisation, and scoring philosophy for the niche securitisation sector. What we kept from Karpathy's approach: • Squarified treemap layout (Bruls et al., 2000) with area proportional to employment • Canvas-based interactive rendering with hover tooltips • Multiple colour layers toggling between different metrics • 0-10 AI exposure scoring scale with calibration anchors • The core insight that AI exposure ≠ job elimination What we changed and why: 1. Data source: BLS scraping → curated taxonomy Karpathy scrapes 342 occupations from the Bureau of Labor Statistics (real government data with exact headcount and pay). We manually defined ~51 securitisation-specific roles because BLS categories are too broad — "Financial Analysts" covers everything from equity research to structured finance. Our headcount and salary estimates are approximations, not census data. 2. Scoring: LLM API calls → pre-generated estimates Karpathy sends each occupation's BLS description to an LLM (Gemini Flash via OpenRouter) with a scoring rubric, generating scores programmatically. We pre-generated all scores and rationales using Claude (Anthropic) since our dataset is smaller and curated. The scoring rubric is similar but adapted. 3. Key heuristic: "digital vs physical" → "routine vs expert judgment" Karpathy's main signal: "If the job can be done from home on a computer, exposure is 7+." This works for the broad economy (roofers 0/10 vs coders 9/10). In securitisation, every role is digital — there are no roofers. So our differentiator is: "routine rule application" (high exposure) vs "bespoke deal judgment and relationships" (lower exposure). 4. Colour layers: BLS Outlook → Automation Risk + Regulatory Complexity Karpathy uses BLS-projected employment growth outlook. We replaced it with two sector-specific metrics: • Automation Risk (0-10): distinct from exposure — measures elimination probability, not transformation • Regulatory Complexity (0-10): unique to our analysis — captures how regulation shapes and protects roles. This is critical in securitisation where post-2008 regulation (EU STS, Basel III, Solvency II) created entire job categories. 5. Market scope: US national → EU/UK securitisation Karpathy covers the entire US economy. We focus on the European/UK securitisation ecosystem with salaries in EUR (converted from GBP at ~1.17), referencing EU/UK regulation (EU STS, CRR, PRA, FCA). 6. Salary data: BLS exact figures → researched market estimates Karpathy uses official BLS median pay data (exact, authoritative). We researched from Robert Half, Morgan McKinley, Glassdoor, eFinancialCareers, and Canary Wharfian (2025-2026 guides) — more approximate but specific to structured finance roles. About the scoring methodology
AI Exposure (0-10): How much will AI reshape this role? In securitisation, the key differentiator is "routine vs expert judgment" rather than "digital vs physical" since nearly all roles are digital. Anchors: - 0-2: Roles with heavy relationship/negotiation focus (structured credit sales) - 3-4: Mix of relationships and analysis (portfolio manager) - 5-6: Significant analysis requiring deal-specific judgment (deal structurer) - 7-8: Heavy analytical/documentation work (compliance, rating analyst) - 9-10: Standardised processing, data entry, reporting Automation Risk (0-10): Likelihood of significant headcount reduction within 5-10 years. Distinct from AI Exposure — a role can be transformed (high exposure) without being eliminated (low automation risk). Regulatory Complexity (0-10): How much is this role driven by regulation (EU STS, Basel III, Solvency II, CRR, MiFID II, AMLD)? Higher scores indicate roles that exist primarily because of regulatory requirements. Scoring approach inspired by Karpathy's US Job Market Visualizer (karpathy.ai/jobs) and informed by academic research on AI occupational exposure (Eloundou et al., 2023; Felten et al., 2023).
Data sources & methodology
IMPORTANT: Transparency note All data in this dashboard (headcount, salaries, scores, rationales) was generated by an AI model (Claude, Anthropic) based on its training knowledge of the securitisation industry. It was NOT extracted from specific proprietary databases or live surveys. The estimates are informed by the sources listed below but should be treated as illustrative approximations for exploratory purposes, not as authoritative market data. Headcount estimates — approximate order-of-magnitude estimates for the EU/UK securitisation market, informed by: • AFME (Association for Financial Markets in Europe) — Securitisation Data Reports & European Structured Finance market sizing: afme.eu/publications/data-research • ECB — Statistical Data Warehouse, euro area securitisation statistics: sdw.ecb.europa.eu • SIFMA — Capital Markets Fact Book, global structured finance issuance data: sifma.org/resources/research/fact-book • Bank of England — Financial Stability Reports, UK securitisation market data: bankofengland.co.uk/financial-stability • LinkedIn Economic Graph — job posting volumes and role distribution patterns for structured finance in EU/UK Salary data — median estimates in EUR for EU/UK markets (GBP converted at ~1.17). Salaries represent base compensation for mid-career professionals; trading/IB roles may earn 50-100%+ additional in bonuses.
